Why it works

Most writing habits die because the bar is too high. A blank page that expects a masterpiece is easy to avoid forever.

Three hundred words is roughly one page — about ten minutes. Short enough that you can always find the time, long enough to get past the throat-clearing and reach the thought you didn't know you had. Do it once and it's a nice morning. Do it every day and it compounds into something real: a clearer head, sharper thinking, and a written record of the person you were becoming.

No prompts to perform for. No audience. No algorithm. Just you, a page, and a number to hit before you close the tab.

How it works

Three steps. Then repeat
tomorrow.

No setup, no configuration, no streak-anxiety dashboards. Open the page and write.

Open a blank page

One clean sheet, one cursor. No formatting toolbars, no notifications, nothing to tweak. Just somewhere quiet to think out loud.

Write until 300

A calm counter fills as you type. Whatever's on your mind counts — a worry, a plan, a memory, a rant. Stop when the goal is met.

Keep the streak alive

Hit 300 and today is marked. Come back tomorrow and the streak grows. Miss a day — it's fine, the page is still here for you.
